Thomas Edison

The man who invented the twentieth century

Dubbed "The Wizard of Menlo Park", he was one of the first inventors to apply the principles of mass production and large-scale teamwork to the process of invention, and because of that, he is often credited with the creation of the first industrial research laboratory.

Here's a time line of Edision's life:

  • Feb 11 1847 - Thomas Edison was born on February 11, 1847 in the canal town of Milan, Ohio, the last of seven children.

  • 1866 - Edison became a telegraph operator after he saved three-year-old Jimmie MacKenzie from being struck by a runaway train.

  • 1867 - At the age of 19, Thomas Edison moved to Louisville, Kentucky, where, as an employee of Western Union, he worked the Associated Press bureau news wire.

  • Jun 1 1869 - Edison's first patented invention was a vote recorder for use by legislative bodies such as Congress.

  • Dec 25 1871 - Mary Stilwell was born in Newark, New Jersey on September 6, 1855, the daughter of Nicholas Stilwell and Margaret Crane.

  • 1876 - Edison allegedly bought light bulb U.S. patent 181,613 of Henry Woodward that was issued August 29, 1876 and obtained an exclusive license to Woodward's Canadian patent.

  • Aug 12 1877 - August 12, 1877, is the date popularly given for Thomas Alva Edison's completion of the model for the first phonograph, a device that recorded sound onto tinfoil cylinders.

  • Feb 19 1878 - The phonograph was developed as a result of Thomas Edison's work on two other inventions, the telegraph and the telephone.

  • Nov 4 1879 - Thomas Edison began serious research into developing a practical incandescent lamp in 1878.

  • 1880 - Edison patented a system for electricity distribution in 1880, which was essential to capitalize on the invention of the electric lamp.

  • Dec 17 1880 - The Edison Illuminating Company was established by Thomas Edison on December 17, 1880, to construct electrical generating stations, initially in New York City.

  • 1882 - In 1882 Tesla moved to Paris, to work as an engineer for the Continental Edison Company, designing improvements to electric equipment brought overseas from Edison's ideas.

  • 1883 - Edison & Swan United Electric Light Company is Established

  • Aug 9 1884 - On December 25, 1871, Edison married 16-year-old Mary Stilwell, whom he had met two months earlier as she was an employee at one of his shops.

  • 1885 - Tesla claims he was offered US$50,000 (~ US$1.1 million in 2007, adjusted for inflation) if he redesigned Edison's inefficient motor and generators, making an improvement in both service and economy.

  • Feb 24 1886 - On February 24, 1886, at the age of thirty nine, Edison married 20-year-old Mina Miller in Akron, Ohio.
